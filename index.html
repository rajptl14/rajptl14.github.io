About Me

I’m an incoming freshman at the University of Wisconsin–Madison studying Molecular and Cellular Biology. Before college, I attended the Illinois Mathematics and Science Academy (IMSA), where I combined rigorous STEM coursework with research in computational biology and medicine. Over the past several years, I have conducted research with Northwestern University's Klein Lab and the University of Illinois Chicago's Minshall Lab, contributed to independent research projects with peers, and explored how computational methods can be used to better understand human disease. Beyond research, I co-founded AURA, a student-led initiative dedicated to supporting breast cancer awareness and early detection. My interests lie at the intersection of biology, computation, and medicine particularly in early disease detection, biological pattern discovery, and the development of computational tools that uncover meaningful insights from complex scientific and medical data. I am also passionate about science communication and enjoy translating technical concepts into ideas that are accessible, engaging, and impactful for broader audiences.
